4:  When we kill some process, it won't kill immediately, it says what signal do you want to send to this process. and this

    process.

7: ps
    --> By defauly it shows us all of the commands and all of the current running processes by root user(only the root user). 
    ("ps" --> stands for process status)

8: ps a
    --> Showing the processes for all of the users on the system, not just the root user

9: ps au
    --> u stands for is displayed the owner or the user associated with a process

10: ps aux
    --> x stands for is showing the processes that are not attached to the terminal, these are system processes
